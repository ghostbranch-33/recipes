# Smoky Cherry Balsamic Glazed Chicken

> Tart cherries are one of the highest-antioxidant foods available. This savory-sweet glaze makes dinners feel special with zero extra effort midweek.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes  
**Cook time:** 25 minutes  
**Servings:** 4  
**Storage:** Refrigerates 4 days

---

## Ingredients

**For the chicken:**
- 1.5 lbs boneless skinless chicken breast
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- ¾ tsp salt
- ¼ tsp black pepper

**For the cherry balsamic glaze:**
- ½ cup frozen or fresh tart cherries, pitted
- 3 tbsp balsamic vinegar
- 1 tbsp raw honey
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- ½ tsp cinnamon

## Instructions

1. **Make the cherry balsamic glaze.** In a small saucepan over medium heat, combine cherries, balsamic vinegar, honey, garlic, smoked paprika, and cinnamon. Simmer 8–10 minutes, stirring and smashing the cherries, until thickened to a glaze. Set aside to cool slightly.

2. **Season and sear.** Pat chicken dry. Season with salt and pepper. Heat olive oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering.

3. **Sear the chicken.** Sear 3 minutes per side until golden brown. Work in batches if needed — don't crowd the pan.

4. **Glaze and finish in oven.** Brush glaze generously over the seared chicken. Transfer skillet to a 400°F oven and roast 12–15 minutes, until internal temperature reaches 165°F.

5. **Rest and store.** Rest 5 minutes, then slice against the grain. Store with extra glaze spooned over top to keep moist.

---

## Serving Ideas

- Sliced over farro or wild rice with wilted spinach
- Alongside roasted Brussels sprouts or broccolini
- Over arugula with walnuts, goat cheese, and a balsamic drizzle
- With mashed cauliflower and roasted garlic

## Why It's Anti-Inflammatory

| Ingredient | Benefit |
|---|---|
| Tart (Montmorency) cherries | Anthocyanins target the same pathways as NSAIDs; well-studied for joint and muscle inflammation |
| Balsamic vinegar | Polyphenols support gut health and reduce oxidative stress |
| Smoked paprika | Rich in carotenoids including capsanthin |
| Cinnamon | Cinnamaldehyde inhibits inflammatory cytokines |
| Garlic | Allicin and sulfur compounds reduce inflammatory markers |
